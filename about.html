About

I am a PhD student in Sociology at Indiana University with an M.S. in Applied Statistics. My research interests include immigration, politics, religion, race and ethnicity, and gender. My dissertation investigates the emergence of a shared group identity in adolescence for Muslim descendants of immigrants in Europe, and it considers how this shared group identity, can, in turn, be used for political engagement and incorporation in adulthood. I am also working on a project investigating if young men and women children of immigrants in the US follow different paths in identity formation between early adolescence and young adulthood.
